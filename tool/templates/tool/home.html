What a Viva Looks Like

A structured, text-based viva delivered through a clean chat interface. The AI asks outcome-aligned questions based on the student's submitted work.

Time Remaining: 08:15
In your essay you argue that rewilding improves ecological resilience. What specific mechanisms support that claim?
One mechanism is trophic cascade restoration. For example, reintroducing predators helps regulate herbivore populations, which reduces overgrazing.
That’s a good start. Could you explain how those cascading effects contribute to long-term ecosystem stability?
By balancing population sizes, vegetation recovers, which stabilises soil and increases biodiversity. This makes the system more resilient to shocks.
What do you consider the strongest counter-argument against rewilding in densely populated regions of the UK?
Probably concerns about land-use conflict — especially where communities rely on intensive agriculture or livestock grazing.
